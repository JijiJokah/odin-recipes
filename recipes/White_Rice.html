How to not screw up Rice, a general guide (FINGER METHOD)

A nice, steaming, hot bowl of white rice. Lightly seasoned, ready to go

One of the biggest obstacles to cooking the perfect batch of fried rice is making white rice in the first place. Today, there shall be none of that. No one will be able to shame you any longer after following this recipe to a T. Get ready to be adopted into an Asian family, because everyone will want you. With that being said, I hope you enjoy this guide written by a fellow white rice fanatic.

What you will need:

  • White rice
  • 2 quartz pot
  • Oil
  • Water

Note: Before you cook your rice, it is important to first wash rinse the rice to remove excess starches. It is said to assist in cooking process

Additionally, it is important to measure a correct rice to water ratio. For this recipe, we use the finger method as it serves for most general uses given a small pot.

Follow these steps for a precise measurement, if not skip to the recipe portion.

  1. Pour rinsed rice into the pot of choosing. Level the rice, and stick your finger in the middle
  2. Make a mental note or mark where the rice level touches your finger segment as seen in the photo
  4. Now move the marker to the equivalent of one finger segment. This is where you should fill the water till. See picture below

Skipping the above

  1. Turn stove top to high. Place rice pot with rice on stove top, and wait for water to begin boiling
  2. Give the rice a quick stir, then cover the rice, and reduce to a low simmer.
  3. Allow to cook for 12-15 minutes
  4. The rice should be well cooked at this point. Remove from simmer, and give it a quick stir to fluff.
  5. Add dry seasoning if you wish.

And that's it! It is quite easy to make beginnner rice, but difficult to master. Keep practicing young chefs!
